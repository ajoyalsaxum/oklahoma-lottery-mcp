# Oklahoma Administrative Rules GPT — User Guide

This GPT gives you live, searchable access to the **Oklahoma Administrative
Code (OAC)** — the official rules and regulations issued by all 177 Oklahoma
state agencies. The same content lives at [rules.ok.gov/code](https://www.rules.ok.gov/code),
but instead of navigating a website you can ask questions in plain English.

---

## Who this is for

- **Lottery retailers and vendors** — compliance requirements, licensing rules,
  game procedures
- **Legal and compliance professionals** — finding and citing specific regulations
- **State agency staff** — looking up rules across agencies quickly
- **Researchers and journalists** — understanding what the regulations actually say
- **Members of the public** — understanding their rights and obligations under
  Oklahoma administrative law

---

## How to ask good questions

### Be specific about the topic

The more specific your question, the more precise the answer.

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---|---|
| "What are the lottery rules?" | "What are the rules for claiming a prize over $600?" |
| "Tell me about health regulations" | "What are the licensing requirements for a home health agency?" |
| "What does DHS say about foster care?" | "What are the background check requirements for foster parents under Title 340?" |

### Name the agency if you know it

Oklahoma's 177 agencies each have their own title number. Mentioning the
agency helps the GPT search in the right place.

| Agency | Title |
|---|---|
| Oklahoma Lottery Commission | 429 |
| State Department of Health | 310 |
| Department of Human Services | 340 |
| Insurance Department | 365 |
| Oklahoma Tax Commission | 710 |
| Corporation Commission | 165 |
| Oklahoma Medical Marijuana Authority | 442 |
| Department of Wildlife Conservation | 800 |
| Department of Labor | 380 |
| Department of Corrections | 170 |

You don't need to memorize numbers — just say "Health Department" or "DHS"
and the GPT will find the right title.

### Ask for citations

Always ask for the OAC citation so you can verify the rule yourself.

> "What is the rule on retailer license suspension, and what's the citation?"

Citations look like `429:10-3-5`, meaning Title 429, Chapter 10, Part 3,
Section 5. You can look up any citation directly at
[rules.ok.gov/code](https://www.rules.ok.gov/code).

## Tips for best results

### 1. Ask follow-up questions
The GPT remembers the conversation. If it finds a relevant rule, you can ask:

- "What are the penalties for violating that rule?"
- "Are there any exceptions to that requirement?"
- "Can you find the full text of the section it referenced?"
- "What other rules in that chapter are related to this?"

### 2. Request the full rule text
If a summary doesn't have enough detail, ask:

> "Can you show me the exact text of that section?"

The GPT will fetch and display the complete regulatory language.

### 3. Search across all agencies
Some topics are regulated by multiple agencies. Ask:

> "Are there rules about data privacy across any Oklahoma state agency?"

> "Which agencies have rules about background checks?"

### 4. Navigate from broad to specific
Start broad, then drill down:

1. "Show me the chapters under the Health Department rules."
2. "What sections are in the Environmental Health chapter?"
3. "Show me the full text of the swimming pool inspection rule."

### 5. Ask for comparisons
> "How does Oklahoma's lottery retailer licensing compare to what's required under the lottery game rules?"

## What the GPT can and cannot do

### It CAN:
- Search and retrieve any rule in the full Oklahoma Administrative Code
- Quote the exact regulatory text
- Navigate the rule tree from agency → chapter → section
- Provide OAC citations for every answer
- Explain what a rule says in plain language

### It CANNOT:
- Tell you how a rule has been applied in a specific court case
- Provide legal advice or tell you whether a rule applies to your situation
- Access rules that haven't been published to the official OAC
- Show proposed rules that haven't been finalized
- Access Oklahoma statutes (those are laws passed by the Legislature,
  not administrative rules)

## Understanding OAC citations

Every rule has a citation in this format:

```
429 : 15 - 1 - 14
 ↑     ↑   ↑   ↑
 │     │   │   └─ Section number
 │     │   └───── Part number
 │     └───────── Chapter number
 └─────────────── Title number (agency)
```

So `429:15-1-14` means:
- **Title 429** — Oklahoma Lottery Commission
- **Chapter 15** — Lottery Games
- **Part 1** — General provisions
- **Section 14** — This specific rule

---

## Rules vs. Statutes — an important distinction

This GPT covers **administrative rules** (the OAC), not **statutes** (laws
passed by the Oklahoma Legislature). The difference matters:

| | Administrative Rules (OAC) | Oklahoma Statutes |
|---|---|---|
| Who creates them | State agencies | Oklahoma Legislature |
| Where to find them | rules.ok.gov/code | oscn.net or sos.ok.gov |
| What they do | Detail how laws are implemented | Create the laws themselves |
| This GPT covers them | **Yes** | **No** |

For statutes, visit [oscn.net](https://www.oscn.net) or
[sos.ok.gov](https://www.sos.ok.gov).
